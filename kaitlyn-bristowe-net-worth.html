Introduction

Kaitlyn Bristowe is a Canadian reality TV personality, YouTuber, podcaster, and entrepreneur.

She rose to prominence when she was selected as a contestant on the American reality shows The Bachelor, The Bachelorette, and Dancing with the Stars.

Bristowe has spoken at high-profile events such as TEDx and sheds light on the negative side of reality TV.

The Origin Story

On June 19, 1985, Kaitlyn was born in Leduc, Alberta, Canada, a rural countryside town.

She was raised by her mother, a ballerina, and her stepfather.

She participated in dance classes as a young child and eventually relocated to Vancouver, Canada, on a dance scholarship.

She recalls how difficult it was for her, arriving with a hockey bag full of clothes, with no friends, nowhere to live, and no money.

After a short time living there, she relocated to Eastern Canada and eventually to Germany with her hockey player boyfriend.

The relationship would ultimately fail, and she moved back in with her mom in Leduc.

Her family convinced her to move back to Vancouver to chase her dreams, and she did just that.

In order to make ends meet in Vancouver, she worked as a hostess at the Cactus Club, a Canadian-owned chain of premium casual restaurants.

She eventually worked her way up to General Manager.

While this was the furthest from her dreams of dancing and performing, she was humbled and appreciated that it paid her bills.

The Bachelor

@Getty

One of her close friends told her The Bachelor was accepting auditions and encouraged her to give it a shot.

Kaitlyn decided to broaden her horizons, so she auditioned for the nineteenth season of the hit reality TV show.

She didn’t hear back, assuming they didn’t like her video.

One year later, she received a voicemail from a casting agent, asking if she was still interested.

Kaitlyn accepted and starred in the following season’s show.

The first episode aired on January 5, 2015.

She placed third overall, making it to week nine, at which point she was eliminated.
